## How teams use Claude for PowerPoint

Available on all paid plans.

### Build from a template

Start with your client or corporate template open, then describe what you need. "Create a market sizing section with TAM, SAM, SOM." Claude generates slides using the correct, on-brand layouts, fonts, and colors from the master deck.

### Iterate on an existing deck

Select a slide and tell Claude what to change. Simplify the text, add a chart, restructure the storyline. Claude makes edits while preserving your formatting and surrounding context.

### Generate a full deck from a description

Open a blank deck and describe your goal. “10 slides walking through an assessment of X industry.” Claude builds a draft with logical structure. Then refine. "Combine slides 5 and 6."

### Create native charts and diagrams

Convert bullet points into professional visuals — diagrams, process flows, or editable native PowerPoint charts. Claude produces visuals you can edit directly, not static images.

## FAQ

### Where is this available?

Claude for PowerPoint is available in beta as a research preview for Pro, Max, Team and Enterprise plan customers.

### Does Claude understand my templates?

Yes. Claude reads the slide master, layouts, fonts, and color scheme in your deck and uses them when generating or editing slides. It won't introduce off-brand elements.

### What are skills and how do I use them in PowerPoint?

Skills let you save a multi-step workflow as a repeatable, one-click action. Save your team's best deck-building processes as a skill that anyone can run directly from Claude for PowerPoint.

### What are the security risks I should know about?

Claude for PowerPoint works within your existing security framework. Claude can make mistakes, so you should always review changes before finalizing, especially for critical deliverables. Please find more details on our [Help Center](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/13521390-using-claude-in-powerpoint)

### Are there any limitations to Claude for PowerPoint?

Claude for PowerPoint is currently in beta as a research preview, so it’s best for tasks where you can review and refine the output. Claude can work with core PowerPoint functionality, but some advanced features may not be fully supported yet.
