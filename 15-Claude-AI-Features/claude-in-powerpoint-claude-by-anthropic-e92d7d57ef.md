## How teams use Claude in PowerPoint

Available to Pro, Max, Team and Enterprise plan customers.

### Build from a client or corporate template

Start with your client or corporate template already loaded. Describe what you need — "Create a market sizing section with TAM, SAM, SOM" — and Claude generates slides using the correct layouts, fonts, and colors from the slide master. No off-brand elements.

### Iterate on an existing deck

Select a slide and tell Claude what to change — simplify the text, add a chart, restructure the storyline. Claude makes edits while preserving your formatting and surrounding context.

### Generate a full deck from a description

Open a blank deck and describe your goal — “10 slides walking through an assessment of X industry.” Claude builds a draft with logical structure and professional defaults. Then refine: "Make slide 3 more visual," "Combine slides 5 and 6."

### Create native charts and diagrams

Convert bullet points into professional visuals — diagrams, process flows, or editable native PowerPoint charts. Claude produces visuals you can edit directly, not static images.

## FAQ

### Where is this available?

Claude in PowerPoint is available in beta as a research preview for Pro, Max, Team and Enterprise plan customers.

### What are the security risks I should know about?

Claude in PowerPoint works within your existing security framework. Claude can make mistakes, so you should always review changes before finalizing, especially for critical deliverables. Please find more details on our [Help Center](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/13521390-using-claude-in-powerpoint)

### Does Claude understand my templates?

Yes. Claude reads the slide master, layouts, fonts, and color scheme in your deck and uses them when generating or editing slides. It won't introduce off-brand elements.

### Are there any limitations to Claude in PowerPoint?

Claude in PowerPoint is currently in beta as a research preview, so it’s best for tasks where you can review and refine the output. Claude can work with core PowerPoint functionality, but some advanced features may not be fully supported yet.
