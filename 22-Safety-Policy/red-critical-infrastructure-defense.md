# AI for Critical Infrastructure Defense

Anthropic partnered with Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) to explore AI's defensive application in protecting critical infrastructure from cyber attacks.

Published: January 8, 2026

## The ALOHA System

PNNL researchers created an adaptive generative AI agent called **ALOHA** (Agentic LLMs for Offensive Heuristic Automation) using Claude. The system demonstrated that AI can dramatically accelerate defensive red teaming exercises.

### How It Works

- PNNL developed a "scaffold" for Claude to automate and accelerate adversary emulation
- The scaffold translates natural language prompts into complex attack chains
- Pre-defined code-based "tools" allow the model to take actions on computer networks
- PNNL's estimate: attack reconstruction completed in **3 hours** instead of **multiple weeks**

## Water Treatment Plant Simulation

Using Claude, PNNL researchers emulated cyber attacks on a high-fidelity simulation of a water treatment plant in far less time than a human expert would have taken. This served as a proof of concept for how AI can help cyber defenders iterate faster on red teaming exercises.

## Partnership Benefits

- Laboratory researchers benefit from an advanced LLM for defensive research
- Anthropic subjects its technology to rigorous testing to prevent misuse
- The work demonstrates practical defensive applications of frontier AI capabilities
